~ Introduction ~

üö© When people are asked about their hobby, they often answer with sports and other activities which require a lot of effort. Fortunately, there are also passions that you can exercise from your lazy chair or bed, and no, I'm not talking about gaming. As one could already tell from the site name, I want to talk about the rather unknown hobby of vexillology which is defined as the scientific study of the separation, symbolism and use of flags. However, one does not have to be a scientist to be a vexillologist, or rather a vexillophile, as since 1961 there are people all over the world who do not practice this study professionally, but for relaxation and personal interest. There are even people everywhere who choose a different flag from their collection every day and hang it on a stick or on their balcony. In this way you can let the whole neighborhood enjoy your passion, as long as the flag does not have a negative connotation of course. The purpose of this site is therefore also to spread the term 'vexillology' and to introduce visitors to the site in an interactive way with what I believe to be the best aspects of this rather varied subject.

~ History ~

üìú The term 'vexillology' was first coined in 1959 years ago as a combination of the words 'vexillum' and '-logia'. However, it was only 2 years later that the study was officialized and separated from heraldry by the American Whitney Smith who devoted his life to organizing flag organizations and meetings. Professional vexillologists have been working daily from that historical moment on the underlying sociology of flags, which can lead to a correct interpretation of their designs, colors and dimensions used. Non-professional vexillophiles also contribute to the flag industry and can participate in the biennial International Congress of Vexillology to meet their favorite flag designers or vexillographers, for example.
