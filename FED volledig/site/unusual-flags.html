~ Overview of unusual flags ~

Proportion

Flag of Nepal

You might already know, but this is the official flag of the Asian country Nepal and it is also the only national flag that does not have the shape of a rectangle. The reason for this is that the two triangles symbolize the well-known Himalayas and the 2 major religions of the country: Hinduism and Buddhism. Furthermore, the flag also contains a sun and crescent moon that were actually represented by faces until 1962. The carmine red and the blue border respectively symbolize the rhododendron, the national flower of Nepal, and the peace that the country knows.

Historical

Flag of Venice

This flag is the favorite of many because of its many original aspects: the banner of the Republic of Venice. The most striking thing about this flag is undoubtedly the fringes on the right side that represent the 6 parts of the city, while preventing wind from damaging the main part. This part is characterized by the proud lion that, depending on the version of the flag, poses with another object: The Bible in times of peace and a sword in war. Although the flag is no longer official since the dissolution of the Republic, you can still see it hanging from many balconies or gondolas in Venice.

Emblem

Flag of Sicily

This flag also has an Italian character as it is that of the autonomous island of Sicily. However, many vexillologists consider its design rather terrifying because of the central triskeles symbol with the head of the mythological gorgon Medusa. The number 3 therefore represents the 3 cape points that characterize the area. The symbol is also very similar to that of the flag of the Isle of Man, although there are still significant differences. The flag is colored diagonally in half in red, respectively the colors of the cities of Palermo and Corleone that played an important role at the time of the Sicilian Revolution.

Design

Flag of Little Havana

The official flag of Little Havana since 2016 is a hideous disaster in all areas of vexillology. A combination of 3D flags of the US and Cuba for a 2D flag, a too realistic drawing of a rooster, text on the flag, flags of American countries as a border, a signature, etc. The design adheres to none of the the five basic principles of flag design. The reason for all this is the fact that the flag was designed by "Loco Joe" Carollo, a paranoid and unstable man who has a lot on his criminal record, but he is also a former mayor of Miami, so his horrible design has not been disapproved by anyone.

Symbolism

Liberian county flags

This piece is not about 1 specific flag as it is about all 15 province flags of Libya, of which the flag on the right, that of River Gee county, is a good example. It shows the main feature of all provincial flags, namely that the 'drawings' are inspired by the quilting tradition of Liberia and that the flag of the country itself is always displayed in the top left corner. The purpose of William Tubman, a politician who introduced the flags, was to promote all meaningful entities, but unfortunately this cultural and social character is often misunderstood and the 'childish designs' are often laughed at.

Proportion

Flag of Ohio

The Ohio Burgee, the official flag of the US state of Ohio, is also known for its odd size. As one can see, it is not an everyday rectangle, but it resembles a Nepalese flag stretched across the width. The red, blue and white design with stars of course refers to the US flag and the triangular field symbolizes the hills and valleys of the state. The large circular disk in the center of the triangle forms an 'O' and thus refers to the name of the state. The thirteen stars grouped around the 'O' represent the original states of the United States, and the four stars added to the apex of the triangle symbolize that Ohio was the seventeenth state admitted to the union.

Historical

Former flag of Libya

Until 2011, Libya had perhaps the most boring national flag in the world: a simple green rectangle because at that time it was the only country with a 1-colour flag. The message behind this was the color green universally symbolizes Islam and it also traditionally re-enacted the historical Libyan region of Tripolitania where the capital Tripoli is located. Furthermore, this flag has 2 versions in proportion and it was adopted in 1977 by the then and well-known leader Gaddafi who thought that the banner fit well with his political philosophy described in 'The Green Book'. After its abolition, the flag is still used by Gaddafi loyalists.

Emblem

Flag of Zheleznogorsk

Many vexillologists would agree if I said that the flag of the Russian city of Zheleznogrosk is a gem of a flag. Despite the fact that the design is not so easy to draw and thus breaks the first rule of flag design, the design is very distinctive and full of symbolism. The city in the Krasnoyarsk Krai region is known for its developed nuclear industry and was nicknamed Krasnoyarsk-26 because of a secret production of plutonium for weapons. Hence, a bear, a typical Russian animal, tries to split open the nucleus of an atom on the flag.

Design

Flag of the FAA Alliance

Created to support the French, British and American Allied Forces (thus the French-Anglo-American Alliance) in WWI, this flag, despite its very beautiful message, is also known for its terrible design among the online vexillology community. It is often described as a fusion of the 3 flags rather than a combination where the emphasis is clearly on the 'The Old Glory' because the 'The Union Jack' and 'Tricolore' are just the stripes and stars of it. For flag collectors, this copy is a real must-have as only 15-20 official copies are estimated to have been made.

Symbolism

Flag of Antarctica

Over the years, there have been many proposals for an official flag for Antarctica, such as Graham Bartram's 1996 proposal and Whitney Smith's 1978 proposal. However, it was Evan Townsed's flag that was the only one recognized by the members of the ATS. This design is now known as 'True South' and is divided into parts with the colors white and navy blue that represent the polar nights and days. In the center there is a diamond, the white part of which symbolizes the icebergs and pressure ridges of Antarctica and the blue part of which is a south-facing compass arrow.
