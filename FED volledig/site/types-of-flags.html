~ Summary ~

Introduction to flag types

You may have already noticed, but in everyday life we are constantly surrounded by flags. Just think of parties, football matches, embassies in big cities, and so on. They can serve as a symbol of unity, as signal elements or simply as decoration. There are in total so many types of flags that it is almost impossible to discuss them all on 1 simple webpage. That is why we will focus on the following 3 categories.

Territorial flags
Flags belonging to geographical areas ranging from villages to planets.
Flags of associations
Flags that stand as a symbol for certain groups of people
Useful flags
Flags that have a practical function in everyday life

~ Territorial flags ~

National flags

Since 2400 BC, man has marked his territory with the use of flags that symbolize the ruling group of that area. For example, the current Scottish flag has been around since the year 832 and the tricolor of Belgium, my own homeland, only came 1000 years later with the revolution. At the time of writing, October 22 2022, 196 states and their flags have been recognized internationally, with the country and flag of South Sudan being the newest.

Flags of micronations

Territorial regions do not need to be recognized by others to declare themselves as a nation. In such instances we talk about micronations and there are a lot of them all over the world. Some were created as a joke like the Republic of Molossia that only includes 1 house, and others take themselves very seriously selling official passports, honorary titles and parcels. This is again the case with the Principality of Sealand. Most of these micronations also have their own flags with some being more professional than others.

DID YOU KNOW?

This boat platform, the Principality of Sealand, only has a population of ±5 people.

International flags

Countries can also cooperate and in this way form multinational organizations and this naturally also includes appropriate flags. Well-known examples are those of the former Soviet Union and that of the still existing United Nations. The former and immense colonial empire of Great Britain also has a legacy due to the watermark on flags such as those of Australia and Tuvalu. This shows once again that unity and sovereignty do not have to be contradictory in all areas.

Planetary flags

Right across from the tiny micronations, we have planets that are part of our galaxy because yes, these too have a flag. Although in principle they cannot be made official as the planets are not inhabited and the flag designs are therefore all unrecognized, there are still a lot of vexillologists who are concerned with this category and especially with designing a flag for the perhaps soon to be inhabited planet Mars. ~ Flags of associations ~

Sports flags

As said before, we are surrounded daily by flags and these are usually those of the organizations that one is affiliated with, such as a sports club. This association species is known for its abundance of tifos and flags. This is especially the case with hard core team sports such as those of the German football club Borussia-Dortmund who are known for their beautiful tifos or the Belgian club with the same colors of which I myself am a big supporter: Lierse K.

Political flags

Whether they are in power or not, many political parties are also characterized by a coat of arms or banner. This can bring supporters together through patriotism, which is the case, for example, with the party of 'Vlaams Belang' and the flag of Flanders. Such a flag can also be characterized by the cultural views of the party or country, which is very clear with the flag of the Qing Dynasty, which has a triangular variant and is also called the 'Yellow Dragon Flag' because of the predominant color which fits very well with the culture of the country.

DID YOU KNOW?

There are still 3 nations whose flags have dragons on them: Wales, Malta and Bhutan.

Flags of genders

We live in the twenty first century so it has become a requirement that everyone has to be accepted and that of course also applies to this site. That is why I would also like to mention the flags of all different genders and/or sexualities ranging from the very specific dreamsexualism to the very broad pansexualism. Although I have to admit that I don't know much about it, I assume that everyone is familiar with the Pride flag, which has changed many times over the years and stands as a symbol for the LGBTQIA+.

~ Useful flags ~

Racing flags

It is a rising sport that is currently taking the upper hand in countries such as the Netherlands and Suriname due to the phenomenal performance of the Dutch-Belgian Max Verstappen, but all this would not have been true if there were no clear agreements about signaling in the sport. And yes, this too is governed by flags such as the red-yellow striped flag indicating a slippery road surface or the black-orange banner indicating an injured car or driver. Yet the black and white checkered finish flag remains the most recognizable for many.

Flags in referanda

It often happens that a region or organization wants to change its flag and that they want to make it an interactive event. This is how official flag elections come about where every resident or member can submit their own design and democracy decides which flag is elected (often after another intervention by an appointed jury to avoid hilarities). A well-known flag that has been elected in this way is the one of the US state of Mississippi that was officially ratified in early 2021. A similar referendum was also held in New Zealand, but there was no winner, so the flag remained the same.

DID YOU KNOW?

The 'Laser Kiwi Flag' was an official proposition in the New Zealand flag referendum.

Peace flags

In times of war or in times of peace, there were always flags that wanted to convey a message. Even if you now walk through the streets of Italy, you will see a rainbow flag with the term 'PACE', the Italian word for peace, hanging on almost every balcony. And the white flag is also known to everyone as the symbol of surrender and this does not go back to the French Revolution as many think, but to the ancient Roman Empire where the historian Cornelius Tacitus already mentioned the use of the white flag.
